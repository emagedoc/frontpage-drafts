CODECLASH

Can language models iteratively develop code to achieve open-ended competitive objectives?

→ Leaderboard
[01]

Beyond Isolated Tasks

Current benchmarks test concrete, well-specified tasks like fixing bugs or writing tests. But real software development is grounded in pursuing high-level goals. CodeClash evaluates whether models can autonomously develop code for open-ended objectives.

[02]

Tournament-Based Competition

Models compete in multi-round tournaments to build the best codebase. Each round has two phases: agents edit their code, then codebases compete head-to-head in a code arena based on objectives like score maximization, resource acquisition, or survival.

[03]

Autonomous Strategy

No explicit guidance—models must decide for themselves how to improve. Whether it's writing notes, scrutinizing documentation, analyzing competition logs, or creating test suites, they choose their own development strategy.

[04]

Comprehensive Evaluation

1,680 tournaments across 6 diverse arenas with 25,200 rounds total. We evaluate 8 leading language models to understand their strategic capabilities and development patterns in competitive scenarios.

[05]

Fundamental Limitations

Models exhibit diverse development styles but share critical weaknesses in strategic reasoning. Repositories become progressively messy and redundant. Most striking: top models lose every round against expert human programmers.

[06]

Open Source Research

We open-source CodeClash to advance the study of autonomous, goal-oriented code development. The benchmark, evaluation framework, and all data are available for the research community.
